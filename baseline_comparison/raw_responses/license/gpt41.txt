### **KEY RISKS & CONSIDERATIONS**

#### **For Licensee**
1. **Improvements assignation (Section 3.2):**
   - Licensee forfeits ownership of improvements, even those developed independently at its own cost.
   - Suggest negotiating a license-back or co-ownership.

2. **Minimum Royalties (Section 4.1):**
   - Minimum royalty payments are due irrespective of sales.
   - Ensure your business can comfortably meet these obligations even in the absence of strong sales.

3. **Exclusive License – Sublicensing Limitation (Section 2.2):**
   - Restricts Licensee’s ability to sublicense. Review your business model and, if sublicensing is important, request more flexibility or automatic approval for certain overrides.

4. **Liability Cap (Section 12.2):**
   - In the event of a defect or title problem with the IP, Licensor’s liability is very limited.
   - Consider requesting carve-outs for willful misconduct, fraud, or gross negligence.

5. **Effect of Termination (Section 13.3):**
   - No license back for improvements or any transition rights.
   - **Sell-off period** is only 90 days – may be short depending on inventory cycles.

6. **Definition gaps:**
   - **Licensed IP** and **Territory** undefined in current draft – need to see Exhibits before execution.

#### **For Licensor**
1. **Audit Provisions:**
   - Audit only once per year. Consider if more frequent audits are reasonable for the size of Licensee’s sales.

2. **Enforcement Rights:**
