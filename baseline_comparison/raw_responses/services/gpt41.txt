### ARTICLE-BY-ARTICLE ANALYSIS

#### ARTICLE 1 - DEFINITIONS
- **Definitions are clear and sensible.** Consider adding definitions for “Confidential Information” and “Affiliate” if relevant for possible later scenarios.

#### ARTICLE 2 - SERVICES
- **Clear, but consider:** Add a provision clarifying whether unsigned SOWs, or email confirmations, can count as executed SOWs, especially for ongoing/ad hoc work.
- **Change Orders:** Ensure both parties understand what constitutes a valid change order—sometimes trivial changes get argued later.

#### ARTICLE 3 - FEES AND PAYMENT
- **Interest Rate:** 1.5% per month is fairly standard. Make sure this complies with local state law (NY generally allows up to this but check usury limits).
- **Deposits:** Standard, but clarify if deposit is refundable if project is canceled prior to work starting.

#### ARTICLE 4 - CLIENT RESPONSIBILITIES
- **Delays:** The notion of timeline extensions and additional fees is good, but it’s wise to spell out how much notice Agency needs to give Client before billing extra for delay, and require documentation.
- **Representative:** Make sure the authority of the Client’s representative is specified (e.g., may sign off, commit resources, etc).

#### ARTICLE 5 - INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY
- **Ownership:** This is a standard “work made for hire after payment” clause. If Client wants IP before full payment, specify so. If Agency wants to re-use generalized elements beyond SOW, clarify.
- **Portfolio Rights:** If Client is in a sensitive business (e.g., pre-launch product, confidential rebrand etc.), add carveouts for confidentiality and approval before public portfolio use.

#### ARTICLE 6 - ACCEPTANCE OF DELIVERABLES
- **Acceptance:** Section 6.1 says that if no written notice is given in 15 business days, the Deliverable is “deemed rejected” **and Agency can cure**. Normally, silence should be treated as acceptance (so-called “acceptance by silence”); “deemed rejected” is unusual and potentially problematic for Agency. Consider changing to: “If Client fails to respond within that period, the Deliverable will be deemed accepted.”
- **Revisions:** It’s good to specify a revision limit, but note that disagreements may arise as to what constitutes a revision. Consider defining what counts as a “round of revisions.”

#### ARTICLE 7 - THIRD-PARTY MATERIALS
- **Licensing:** Ensure that roles and responsibilities (including duration and territory of use for stock items) are spelled out, and include “pass-through” of license terms to Client.
- **Liability:** Agency should be protected if Client violates terms of a stock asset that Agency properly licensed.

#### ARTICLE 8 - REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES
- **Standard warranties.** Agency should possibly caveat its IP warranty regarding third-party materials or open-source elements (see Section 7).
- **Disclaimer:** Explicit; this is standard.

#### ARTICLE 9 - CONFIDENTIALITY
- 3-year survival is standard but for some highly sensitive info, perpetual protection might be needed (e.g., trade secrets).
- Wide definition of Confidential Information (not stated here, but recommended) would strengthen this clause.

#### ARTICLE 10 - LIMITATION OF LIABILITY
- Both cap and exclusion are **Agency-friendly**. Cap is “the SOW amount,” which is generally fair, but Client may want higher (aggregate) cap, especially for repeated/large projects.
- Consider exceptions for willful misconduct or gross negligence (currently not mentioned).

#### ARTICLE 11 - INDEMNIFICATION
- Agency is only indemnifying on IP grounds regarding Deliverables **excluding** Client Materials, which is standard.
- Client indemnifies Agency for Client Materials, instructions, and misuse—also standard.

#### ARTICLE 12 - TERM AND TERMINATION
- **Termination for Convenience:** Good, but terminating “the MSA” while keeping SOWs running is useful; spell out how long SOWs remain in force after termination (e.g., until completion unless terminated for breach).
- **Effect of Termination:** Agency must deliver all completed and paid-for work product (not all “work product”).

#### ARTICLE 13 - GENERAL PROVISIONS
- **Dispute Resolution:** Mandatory arbitration is standard, but specify if it is administered by a specific body (e.g., AAA) and governing rules; clarify if injunctive relief is available in court for IP, confidentiality, or non-payment issues.
- **Assignment:** Reasonable, but consider allowing assignment to affiliates or in a merger/sale with notice.
- **Amendment:** Writing and signatures are best, but consider whether email exchange suffices for amendments.

### GENERAL SUGGESTIONS / RED FLAGS

1. **Client Contact Person:** Specify that instructions or approvals given by Client’s designated point of contact are binding.
2. **Data Protection:** If Agency will process any personal information on behalf of Client, add privacy/data processing terms to address GDPR/CCPA if relevant.
3. **Force Majeure:** Add a clause excusing obligations in case of events beyond control (e.g., fire, pandemic, war).
4. **Counterparts / Digital Signatures:** Allow for execution in counterparts and via e-signature.
5. **Non-Solicitation:** If Agency wishes to prevent Client from hiring Agency staff for a certain period, this should be included.
6. **Severability and Waiver:** Consider standard legal boilerplate for unenforceability of any part of the contract and that failure to enforce is not waiver.
7. **Survival:** Specify which provisions survive termination (e.g., IP, confidentiality, indemnity, payment).
